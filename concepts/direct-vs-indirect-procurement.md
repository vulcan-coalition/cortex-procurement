# Direct vs Indirect Procurement

This distinction affects both the process and the data model.

## Comparison

| Aspect | Direct Procurement | Indirect Procurement |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------|
| What | Raw materials, components | Office supplies, IT equipment, travel, services |
| Relation to Product | Directly used in production | Not used in production |
| Volume | High volume, recurring | Varied, often ad-hoc |
| Contracts | Long-term, pre-qualified suppliers | Mix of contracts and spot purchases |
| Impact | Affects product cost directly | Affects operating cost |
| Control | Centralized, tightly managed | Often decentralized, harder to control |

## Example from Initial Docs

The IT Equipment purchase (PR-2024-0892) is **Indirect Procurement** — laptops, monitors, headsets, and docking stations for the dev team. These are not used in production but support business operations.
