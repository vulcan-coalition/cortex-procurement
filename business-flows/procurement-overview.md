# Procurement Overview — Procure-to-Pay (P2P) Cycle

## What is P2P?

Procure-to-Pay (P2P) is the end-to-end cycle of procurement, from the moment a need arises until payment is made to the supplier. Every step produces data that flows through the system.

## The 8 Steps of the P2P Cycle

```
Need Identification → Sourcing → Vendor Selection → Contract & Negotiation
       ↓                                                      ↓
  Vendor Measurement ← Invoice & Payment ← Delivery & Receipt ← Purchase Order
```

### Step 1: Need Identification

A department identifies a need for goods or services and creates a **Purchase Requisition (PR)**. The PR specifies what is needed, quantity, budget, and required date. It goes through an internal approval chain before procurement begins.

### Step 2: Sourcing & RFx

The procurement team analyzes the requirement and selects the appropriate sourcing method:
- **RFI** (Request for Information) — market survey to understand vendor capabilities
- **RFQ** (Request for Quotation) — when specs are clear, compare prices directly
- **RFP** (Request for Proposal) — for complex solutions requiring holistic evaluation

See [02-sourcing.md](02-sourcing.md) for details.

### Step 3: Vendor Selection

Evaluate and compare proposals from multiple vendors using predefined criteria. Uses a **Vendor Scorecard / Evaluation Matrix** with weighted scoring to produce an objective, defensible decision.

See [03-vendor-selection.md](03-vendor-selection.md) for details.

### Step 4: Contract & Negotiation

Negotiate terms with the selected vendor covering price, duration, delivery conditions, penalties, and SLAs. Formalize with a **Contract or Framework Agreement**.

See [04-contract-and-negotiation.md](04-contract-and-negotiation.md) for details.

### Step 5: Purchase Order

Issue a formal **Purchase Order (PO)** to the awarded vendor. The PO is a legally binding document specifying items ordered, prices, and delivery terms.

See [05-purchase-order.md](05-purchase-order.md) for details.

### Step 6: Delivery & Receipt

Inspect received goods against the PO for quantity and quality. Record a **Goods Receipt Note (GRN)** to confirm receipt and support 3-Way Matching (PO + GRN + Invoice).

See [06-delivery-and-receipt.md](06-delivery-and-receipt.md) for details.

### Step 7: Invoice & Payment

Finance/AP verifies the **Invoice** against PO and GRN (3-Way Match). If everything matches, payment is approved according to agreed terms.

See [07-invoice-and-payment.md](07-invoice-and-payment.md) for details.

### Step 8: Vendor Measurement

Measure vendor performance using KPIs across delivery, quality, cost, and compliance. This historical performance data feeds back into future sourcing decisions as a feedback loop.

See [08-vendor-measurement.md](08-vendor-measurement.md) for details.

## Key Documents in the P2P Cycle

| Abbreviation | Full Name | Purpose | Created By |
|-------------|-----------|---------|------------|
| PR | Purchase Requisition | Internal purchase request — states need before approval | Requester / End user |
| RFI | Request for Information | Market survey — ask vendors about their capabilities | Procurement Team |
| RFQ | Request for Quotation | Price request — used when specs are clear for direct comparison | Procurement Team |
| RFP | Request for Proposal | Proposal request — used for complex solutions, holistic evaluation | Procurement Team |
| PO | Purchase Order | Official purchase order — legally binding between organization and vendor | Procurement Team |
| GRN | Goods Receipt Note | Delivery confirmation — verifies received goods match the PO | Warehouse |
