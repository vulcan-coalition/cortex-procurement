# Supply Chain Overview

## What is Supply Chain?

Supply Chain is the network of all activities involved in producing and delivering goods or services, from upstream (raw materials / suppliers) to downstream (customers). Three things flow through the system at all times.

## 3 Flows in Supply Chain

### Material Flow (Forward)

Goods and raw materials travel from Supplier → Procurement → Warehouse → Customer.

### Information Flow (Bidirectional)

Demand forecasts, PO confirmations, and status tracking flow back and forth between all nodes.

### Money Flow (Reverse)

Money flows from Customer → Organization → Supplier through the AP (Accounts Payable) system.

## Where Procurement Sits

Procurement is the **gateway** between the organization and the supplier world. It converts internal needs into actual purchase orders and verifies that what was purchased matches what was needed.

- **Behind:** Departments with demand (Operations, IT, Marketing, etc.)
- **In front of:** All suppliers and vendors
- **Connected to:** Finance (payment side) and Legal (contract side)
